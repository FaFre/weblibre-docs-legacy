The **tree view** is a powerful tool for managing and navigating a large number of related tabs. It is especially useful when you:

- **Start with a main page** and open multiple related websites or tabs (e.g. search results).
- **Explore each branch further**, opening additional pages as you go.

With the tree view, **all your tabs are organized hierarchically**, reflecting the path you took from the starting page to each branch. This makes it easy to:

- **Return to the starting page** at any time.
- **Switch between different branches or tabs** without losing your place.
- **Visualize the relationships** between your tabs, so you never lose track of your browsing context.

**Example workflow:**

1. Begin with a search result (root).
2. Open several results (branches).
3. Dive deeper into any result by opening further pages (sub-branches).
4. Use the tree view to easily jump back to the root or switch between branches.

**Benefits:**

- **Efficient navigation** through complex browsing sessions.
- **Clear overview** of how your tabs are related.
- **Quick access** to any page in your exploration tree.
